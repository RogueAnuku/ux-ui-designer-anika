Projects
Revitalizing Compassion
The project proposes the redesign of Roger Neilson House's webpage, a palliative care facility in Ottawa. User research, including interviews and surveys, was conducted to gather insights. Competitor analysis highlighted strengths in accessibility standards, but weaknesses were found in certain websites. Heuristic evaluation identified issues with images, contact information, page length, and font size. User perspectives, actions, feelings, and pain points were considered. The problem statement focused on saving user information to streamline the volunteer application process. Sketches were created, and the site map was adjusted to include an 'About Us' section. A UI style guide was developed based on user preferences. Next steps included incorporating features, adding a contact form, and improving accessibility.
Transforming the Digital Frontier
The non-profit organization's website faced issues with cluttered navigation, outdated information, and limited resource access. Steps taken to address the problem included a heuristic analysis and usability testing. Insights led to a redesign of the information architecture, improvements in the footer design, and the creation of mockups and a UI style guide. The resulting solution aimed to provide a clearer purpose, intuitive navigation, and easier access to resources. Future steps involve finalizing prototypes and perfecting the footer design.
Smart Recipes
This case study addresses the issue of finding personalized recipes. The solution is an AI-powered mobile app that generates recipes based on dietary preferences, available ingredients, and meal types. It aims to save time, expand culinary knowledge, and improve meal planning for users, fostering a seamless and enjoyable experience while helping them learn new recipes, save on groceries, and manage their time effectively.
Give Guardian
The concept of Give Guardian was conceived out of a deep-seated passion. The issue at hand is that individuals are often faced with the daunting and time-consuming task of conducting their research on non-profit organizations (NPOs), which can lead to confusion and expose them to potential scams. The proposed solution involves the development of a web browser extension designed to simplify this process for users, providing added security through the verification of NPOs with a dedicated badge.
About
What sets Anika apart is her unique background, characterized by their experience as a residential program manager for adults with developmental disabilities. This valuable experience has instilled in her a profound sense of empathy and a strong motivation to enhance usability and accessibility for all individuals. She firmly believes that technology possesses the power to bridge gaps and offer equitable opportunities, enabling marginalized voices to be heard. Fueled by this passion, her focus lies in creating designs that are not just visually appealing but also inclusive and accessible.
