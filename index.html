Revitalizing Compassion

Case Study by: JULIE NG, CHIARA FALLANI, RAINA AL HUSSAMI & ANIKA MCPHEE (June 8th, 2023 - June 28th, 2023)

Our team revitalized a not-for-profit organization's website, focusing on improving the user interface (UI) for enhanced engagement. A heuristic evaluation revealed the need for a better UI, crucial for an organization heavily dependent on volunteer support. Research uncovered a key issue: potential volunteers found the application process too complex and lengthy. Our data showed a significant overhaul of the volunteer application flow was necessary to encourage greater user participation

Transforming the Digital Frontier

A independant case study by: Anika McPhee. MAY 15th-31st, 2023

This case study focuses on improving a non-profit website's information hierarchy. Facing issues like cluttered navigation and outdated content, we conducted heuristic analysis and usability testing. The insights led to an overhaul of the information architecture, improved footer design, and the creation of mockups and a UI style guide. The solution aims for clarity, intuitive navigation, and enhanced resource access. Next steps involve finalizing prototypes and refining the footer design.

Case Study

Give Guardian

Give Guardian, a case study by: ANIKA MCPHEE UX DESIGNER CHIARA FALLANI UX DESIGNER DHWANI TRIVEDI UX DESIGNER DESIREE HENRY UX DESIGNER

The Give Guardian concept stems from a passionate desire to simplify the research process on non-profit organizations (NPOs). Individuals often face the challenge of time-consuming research, leading to confusion and potential exposure to scams. Our proposed solution is a web browser extension designed to streamline this process, offering added security by verifying NPOs with a dedicated badge.

About

Anika McPhee is a UX/UI designer who brings a decade of user-focused expertise to her work. Her extensive background in human services, including leadership and management roles, has honed her communication, collaboration, and problem-solving skills. This deep understanding of user needs fuels her passion for designing intuitive, accessible experiences. Anika is proficient in Figma, user research methodologies, and responsive web design principles. She holds a UX/UI Design Certificate from the University of Toronto and a Bachelor of Arts from the University of Ottawa.
